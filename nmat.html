📋What Is NMAT?

NMAT (NMIMS Management Aptitude Test) is a national-level MBA entrance examination conducted by the Graduate Management Admission Council (GMAC). Unlike CAT, NMAT lets candidates choose their preferred exam date, time slot and test mode within the testing window. Candidates may attempt the exam up to three times, and participating institutes consider the highest score.

GMAC is the same organisation behind the GMAT, and that lineage shows in how the test is built. NMAT is a computer adaptive exam, meaning the difficulty of the questions you see shifts in real time based on how you are performing.

✅NMAT Eligibility Criteria

Part of NMAT's appeal is how straightforward its eligibility requirements are.

  • Educational qualificationA bachelor's degree, or equivalent, from a recognised university.
  • Degree durationThe degree should typically be of at least three years' duration.
  • Minimum marksMany participating colleges require a minimum aggregate, often 50 percent, though the exact requirement varies by institution.
  • Final-year studentsGenerally eligible to apply, subject to the individual college's policy.
  • Age limitThere is no prescribed upper age limit for taking NMAT.

Eligibility to sit the exam and eligibility for admission are not the same thing. GMAC allows a broad range of candidates to take NMAT, but each participating institute may add its own academic requirements, sectional score expectations, work experience preferences or interview criteria.

Common eligibility questions

Can final-year students apply? Yes. Most final-year students can register, provided they complete their degree within the timeline specified by the institute they eventually join.

Is work experience mandatory? No. Fresh graduates can apply. Certain executive or specialised MBA programmes may prefer candidates with prior work experience.

Can engineering students appear? Yes. NMAT is open to graduates from engineering, commerce, science, arts, management, law, pharmacy and other recognised disciplines.

🔀How the NMAT Adaptive Format Works

NMAT adjusts question difficulty in real time based on your performance on the previous question, continuously assessing your ability as you move through the paper. This is the single biggest difference between NMAT and every fixed-form exam you have practised for.

The adaptive loop

Initial assessment. The exam opens with a question of moderate difficulty.

If you answer correctly → the next question gets harder.

If you answer incorrectly → the next question gets easier.

Continuous adjustment. The loop repeats throughout the section, tailoring the paper to your individual ability level.

No negative marking. Never leave a question blank.

Because there is no penalty for a wrong answer, an informed guess always beats a skip. And because you cannot revisit a section once its timer ends, the cost of over-thinking early questions is far higher in NMAT than in CAT.

❓Frequently Asked Questions — NMAT 2026

What is a good NMAT score for top MBA colleges?

A score of 220 or above is generally competitive for top colleges such as NMIMS Mumbai. Many other leading B-schools accept scores between 190 and 220, depending on their admission criteria.

How many times can I attempt the NMAT exam?

Up to three times in one admission cycle, including your first attempt and two retakes. Participating institutes consider the highest score.

Is there negative marking in NMAT?

No. NMAT has no negative marking, so it is always better to make an informed attempt than to leave a question blank.

Can final-year students apply for NMAT?

Yes. Final-year undergraduate students are eligible to appear, provided they meet the admission requirements of the colleges they apply to.

Is NMAT easier than CAT?

NMAT is generally considered less difficult than CAT, but its strict sectional timings and adaptive format demand a different preparation strategy. Easier questions do not mean an easier exam.

How long is an NMAT score valid?

An NMAT score is valid only for the current admission cycle. Candidates must appear again if they wish to apply in a future cycle.

Which colleges accept NMAT scores?

More than 50 B-schools accept NMAT, including NMIMS, TAPMI, XIM University (XIMB), KJ Somaiya Institute of Management, SDA Bocconi Asia Center and several other reputed institutes across India.

Are calculators allowed in the NMAT exam?

No. Calculators are not permitted. All calculations must be done manually, so practise solving quantitative problems without one.
