🎯 NMAT 2026 Preparation Strategy
1
Use All 3 Attempts Strategically: Prepare well for your first attempt since NMIMS Mumbai only accepts first-attempt scores. Use retakes for other colleges.
2
Manage Sectional Time Carefully: Each section has a fixed time limit. Once time is up, you move to the next section automatically. Practice with section-wise timers.
3
Focus on Speed in Quant: NMAT Quant is time-intensive. Practice calculation shortcuts and formula-based approaches to finish 36 questions in 52 minutes.
4
Vocabulary is Key in Language Skills: NMAT tests vocabulary more heavily than CAT. Build a word list of 500+ high-frequency NMAT words and revise daily.
5
Schedule Your Exam Early: Since NMAT is adaptive and slot-based, book your slot as soon as registration opens. Early slots have more choice and less pressure.
❓ Frequently Asked Questions — NMAT 2026
How is NMAT different from CAT?
NMAT is adaptive, has no negative marking, and allows candidates to choose their test date. CAT is held on a single day. NMAT is easier than CAT and primarily targets NMIMS and partner B-schools.
Can I retake NMAT if my score is low?
Yes. NMAT allows up to 3 attempts per year with a 15-day gap between retakes. Note: NMIMS Mumbai only considers your first attempt score.
Which colleges accept NMAT scores?
NMIMS Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Indore, Navi Mumbai; KJ Somaiya; TAPMI Manipal; SDA Bocconi Asia Center; XIMB; Woxsen University, and 78+ other institutes.
Is there negative marking in NMAT?
No. NMAT has no negative marking. Attempt every question — even guessing is rewarded.
