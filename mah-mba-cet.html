🎯 MAH MBA CET 2026 Preparation Strategy
1
Logical Reasoning is Everything: LR carries 75 of 200 marks. MAH CET LR includes high-level complex puzzles that take 8–10 minutes each. Practise at least 5 complex LR sets daily.
2
No Negative Marking — Attempt All 200: Since MAH CET has zero negative marking, attempt every single question. Guess intelligently on questions you can partially eliminate options for.
3
Abstract Reasoning Needs Pattern Practice: Abstract reasoning tests visual pattern recognition. Practice series, mirror images, and water images daily — these 25 marks come quickly with practice.
4
150 Minutes for 200 Questions Requires Speed: At 45 seconds per question average, MAH CET rewards speed. Build a habit of reading questions fast and eliminating wrong options quickly.
5
Two Attempts Strategy: Use the first attempt (April) to gauge your level. Identify weak sections and target improvements for the second attempt (May 2026 structure) to maximise your score.
❓ Frequently Asked Questions — MAH MBA CET 2026
Is there a GD/PI round for MAH MBA CET admission?
No. One of the biggest advantages of MAH CET is that seat allotment through CAP rounds is purely merit-based — no Group Discussion or Personal Interview required.
Which is the best college through MAH MBA CET?
JBIMS Mumbai is the most prestigious college admitting students through MAH CET for MBA. SIMSREE and K.J. Somaiya are also top-ranked colleges accepting MAH CET scores.
Can students from outside Maharashtra apply?
Yes. Students from outside Maharashtra can apply under the All India category using CAT/CMAT/MAT/XAT/ATMA scores through the CAP process.
What is the new 2026 change in MAH CET?
2026 introduced a second attempt for the first time. Candidates could appear for both attempts (April and May 2026) and the better score was considered.
