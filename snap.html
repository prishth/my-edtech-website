🎯 SNAP 2026 Preparation Strategy
1
Logical Reasoning is the Backbone: LR carries 25 of 60 questions in SNAP. Focus on speed and accuracy in puzzles, arrangements, and blood relation questions.
2
SNAP English is Vocab-Heavy: Unlike CAT, SNAP English tests vocabulary extensively. Build a strong word bank and practise grammar-based questions daily.
3
Use All 3 Attempts: Take SNAP Test 1 as a dress rehearsal. Analyse your mistakes and improve for Tests 2 and 3. The best score is used.
4
Time Management in 60 Minutes: With 60 questions in 60 minutes, you have exactly 1 minute per question. Never spend more than 90 seconds on any single question.
5
Apply to Multiple Symbiosis Institutes: Pay the ₹1,000 programme fee and apply to 3–4 Symbiosis institutes to maximise your chances of a GE-PI-WAT call.
❓ Frequently Asked Questions — SNAP 2026
Can I appear for SNAP more than once?
Yes. SNAP allows up to 3 attempts within a single year. The best score across all your attempts is considered for admission shortlisting.
Which colleges accept SNAP scores?
Only Symbiosis institutes accept SNAP scores — including SIBM Pune, SCMHRD, SIIB, SITM, SIOM, SSBF, SCIT, and 11 other Symbiosis institutes.
Is SNAP easier than CAT?
Yes. SNAP is considerably easier than CAT. The 60-minute, 60-question format with shorter RC passages and simpler Quant makes it more approachable.
What is a good SNAP score for SIBM Pune?
SIBM Pune is the top SNAP-accepting college. Typically, a SNAP percentile of 98.5+ is required for shortlisting at SIBM Pune.
