🎯 XAT 2026 Preparation Strategy
1
Master Decision Making First: DM is XAT's most unique and challenging section. It tests real-world judgment and has no parallel in CAT. Practice 5 DM sets daily from previous XAT papers.
2
Don't Neglect GK: GK carries 25 questions but is not counted in percentile for most colleges. Spend 20–30 minutes daily on current affairs and static GK.
3
Manage Unattempted Questions: XAT penalises leaving more than 8 questions unattempted. Never leave more than 8 blank — even guess on the 9th question.
4
Solve XAT PYQs: XAT has a distinct question style, especially in Verbal and DM. Solving the last 5 years of XAT papers is the best preparation.
5
Time Management is Critical: 210 minutes sounds like a lot, but with 100+ questions and complex DM sets, time flies. Practice full-length mocks with strict timing.
❓ Frequently Asked Questions — XAT 2026
What makes XAT different from CAT?
XAT has a unique Decision Making (DM) section not found in CAT. It also has a General Knowledge section and is conducted by XLRI Jamshedpur. XAT is accepted by XLRI and 160+ B-schools across India.
How many times can I appear for XAT?
There is no limit on the number of times you can appear for XAT. You can take it every year.
Is there negative marking in XAT?
Yes. There is -0.25 marks for each wrong answer. Additionally, if you leave more than 8 questions unattempted, there is a penalty of -0.10 marks per unattempted question beyond 8.
Which colleges accept XAT scores?
XLRI Jamshedpur, XIMB Bhubaneswar, IMT Ghaziabad, TAPMI Manipal, SP Jain Mumbai, Great Lakes Chennai, and 160+ other top B-schools accept XAT scores.
