🎯 MAT 2026 Preparation Strategy
1
Skip GK for Score, Not for Cutoffs: The Indian & Global Environment section doesn't count toward the composite score. Spend minimal time here during the exam — use saved time for other sections.
2
MAT Mathematical Skills is Fundamental-Based: Questions are easier than CAT. Focus on arithmetic, algebra, and geometry basics. Speed and accuracy matter more than complexity.
3
4 Attempts Per Year is an Advantage: Unlike CAT, you can appear 4 times. Use Feb/May sessions as practice and aim for your best in Sep/Dec when preparation is complete.
4
Attempt MAT with CAT Prep: MAT preparation overlaps significantly with CAT. Students preparing for CAT often appear for MAT as a backup — the syllabus is nearly identical.
5
CBT Mode Recommended: CBT mode allows you to mark and review questions easily. Use the flag feature to revisit difficult questions after finishing the section.
❓ Frequently Asked Questions — MAT 2026
How many times is MAT conducted in a year?
MAT is conducted 4 times a year — in February, May, September, and December. Candidates can appear in multiple sessions.
Is Indian & Global Environment counted for MAT score?
No. The Indian & Global Environment section (40 marks) is NOT counted in the composite MAT score used by colleges. Only the first 4 sections (160 marks) are scored.
What is a good MAT score?
A composite score of 700+ (out of 800) or 90+ percentile is considered good for top MAT-accepting colleges.
Can I appear for MAT multiple times?
Yes. You can appear for MAT across multiple sessions in the same year. Colleges generally consider your best score.
