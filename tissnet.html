🎯 TISSNET 2026 Preparation Strategy
1
For HRM: Treat CAT as Your Primary Exam: Since TISS HRM uses CAT scores, your CAT preparation is your TISS preparation. Aim for 95+ percentile to be competitive for TISS Mumbai HRM.
2
CUET PG Preparation is Domain-Specific: CUET PG tests your domain knowledge. Focus on the specific paper code relevant to your chosen TISS programme. Check the official paper-code list on admissions.tiss.ac.in.
3
Prepare Thoroughly for PAT: PAT is elimination-based and determines who reaches the interview stage. Practise aptitude-based questions, case analyses, and written ability tests.
4
Research Your Programme Thoroughly: TISS offers unique programmes not available elsewhere. Know exactly what each programme covers and why you want it — the OPI tests clarity of purpose.
5
Keep Track of Official Dates: Since admission now follows NTA/IIM timelines, students must check admissions.tiss.ac.in regularly for PAT and OPI schedule announcements.
❓ Frequently Asked Questions — TISSNET 2026
Is TISSNET still conducted?
No. TISSNET has been discontinued from the 2024-25 academic year. TISS now uses CUET PG (for most programmes) and CAT (for HRM and ODCL) for admissions.
Which exam should I appear for TISS HRM admission?
For MA in Human Resource Management & Labour Relations and MA in Organisation Development, Change and Leadership, you must appear for CAT. Other TISS programmes require CUET PG.
What is the TISS PAT?
TISS Programme Aptitude Test (PAT) is a 45-minute aptitude test conducted online after shortlisting based on CUET PG/CAT. It tests programme-specific aptitude and writing skills.
Is TISS programmes equivalent to an MBA?
TISS does not offer an MBA. It offers MA in HRM & LR (similar to MBA in HR), which is highly regarded in the industry and placed at par with top B-school MBA programmes for HR roles.
