🎯 CLAT 2026 Preparation Strategy
1
Focus on Legal Reasoning: Legal Reasoning carries the highest weight (35+ questions). Build strong fundamentals of how legal principles are applied to factual scenarios. No prior law knowledge is needed — principles are given in the question.
2
Read Newspapers Daily: Current Affairs forms 28-32 questions. Read The Hindu or Indian Express daily and maintain a notes file for important events, appointments, and awards.
3
Build Reading Speed: All CLAT sections involve comprehension passages. A high reading speed (500+ words per minute) is crucial to finish the paper in 2 hours.
4
Solve CLAT PYQs: CLAT PYQs from the last 5 years reveal recurring themes in all sections. Download them for free from Prishth and solve under timed conditions.
5
Attempt Mock Tests: Start taking full-length offline mocks 3 months before the exam. Simulate the exact exam conditions including using a pen and paper.
❓ Frequently Asked Questions — CLAT 2026
Is CLAT conducted offline or online?
CLAT is conducted in offline (pen and paper) mode, unlike most other entrance exams which are computer-based.
Can Science or Commerce students appear for CLAT?
Yes, students from any stream — Science, Commerce, or Arts — can appear for CLAT UG. There is no stream restriction.
What is the minimum score required to get into NLSIU Bangalore?
NLSIU Bangalore (Rank 1 NLU) typically requires a CLAT rank within the top 100-200 for General category. The exact cutoff varies each year.
How many attempts are allowed for CLAT?
There is no restriction on the number of CLAT attempts. You can appear every year as long as you meet the eligibility criteria.
Is CLAT difficult for non-PCM students?
CLAT has no Mathematics section in the traditional sense — only basic Quantitative Techniques. Students from Arts and Commerce often perform better due to stronger English and GK backgrounds.
