🔄What Changed in the XAT Exam Pattern

The notification introduced two structural changes that matter when you use older papers for practice:

  • The General Knowledge section now carries 20 questions, reduced from 25.
  • The total examination time is now 180 minutes, reduced from 190 minutes.

Papers from earlier years are still valuable for question quality and difficulty calibration. Just adjust your timing when you attempt them, so your practice matches the current 180-minute constraint.

📊XAT Exam Structure

XAT carries roughly 95 questions to be solved in 180 minutes. The paper is split into two parts: Part 1 covers VALR, Decision Making and QA & DI within a shared 170-minute window, and Part 2 covers General Knowledge in a separate 10-minute block.

🎯Why Solving XAT Previous Year Papers Matters

Past papers let you see the real nature of the questions, the difficulty band and the structure of the paper — things no summary can teach you.

  • Identify repeated patternsMany questions, particularly in QA and Verbal, follow consistent formats year after year.
  • Improve accuracyRepeated exposure builds familiarity, which is what stops you falling for the traps built into tricky options.
  • Simulate exam conditionsSolving a full paper against the clock is the closest you can get to the real pressure of exam day.
  • Track your progressWorking through papers from earlier years gives you a measurable sense of improvement over time.

✅How to Use These Papers

Treat past papers as diagnostic tools, not as a checklist. Attempt each one in a single sitting under timed conditions, then spend at least as long analysing your attempt as you spent taking it. The analysis is where the score comes from.

Aim to cover the last five years first, since those reflect the current pattern most closely, then work backwards for extra practice on question quality.

❓Frequently Asked Questions — XAT Previous Year Papers

How many XAT previous year papers should I solve?

Solve at least the past five years' papers. Those reflect the current pattern most accurately. Older papers are still useful for additional practice on question quality.

Is the difficulty level of XAT consistent across years?

XAT papers generally stay in a moderate to high difficulty band, though individual sections vary from year to year. Decision Making in particular changes character depending on the paper setter.

Are these XAT question papers free to download?

Yes. Every paper on this page is free and requires no sign-up, login or registration.

Can solving past papers alone guarantee a good XAT score?

No. Past papers are a crucial part of preparation but they work best alongside solid conceptual grounding and regular full-length mock tests.

Do older XAT papers still match the current pattern?

Not exactly. GK has been reduced from 25 to 20 questions and total time from 190 to 180 minutes. Adjust your timing when attempting older papers so your practice matches the current constraint.
