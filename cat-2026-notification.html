✅ CAT 2026 Eligibility Criteria
🏫
Academic Qualification Candidates must hold a Bachelor's degree from a recognised university with a minimum of 50% aggregate marks (or equivalent CGPA). SC, ST, and PwD candidates require a minimum of 45% marks.
🅾
Final Year Students Final year Bachelor's degree students or those awaiting results may also apply, subject to approval from their university or institution's Principal / Registrar.
📄
Professional Degree Holders Candidates who have completed CA / CS / ICWA (CMA) / Fellow of the Institute of Actuaries of India (FIAI) with the required percentage can also appear in the CAT exam.
👤
Age Limit There is no upper age limit for the CAT exam, making it accessible to working professionals, fresh graduates, and aspirants of all ages.

📝 CAT 2026 Registration Process — Step by Step

CAT registration is a detailed process. Read this guide carefully before you begin. Use a laptop or desktop with a stable internet connection — avoid using mobile phones for registration.

1

Visit the Official CAT Website

Open a browser (Chrome, Edge, or Firefox) and go to iimcat.ac.in. Click the Register button on the homepage.

2

Create Your Account

Enter your name, email ID, mobile number, city, and a preferred password. Click Submit. A user account is created and you will receive a confirmation on your email.

3

Fill the Application Form

Log in and complete the form with your academic qualifications, work experience, and personal details. Enter everything accurately — errors can lead to disqualification.

4

Upload Required Documents

Upload your passport-size photograph (JPEG, 35mm x 45mm, below 80 KB), signature (JPEG, below 80 KB), mark sheets, degree certificate, caste certificate (if applicable), and ID proof.

5

Select Test Cities and IIMs

Choose up to 4 exam centre cities. Select the IIMs you wish to apply to carefully — you will only be eligible for the WAT-PI process of the IIMs you select in this form.

6

Pay the Application Fee

Pay ₹2,500 (General) or ₹1,250 (Reserved category) via debit card, credit card, or net banking. Save the payment confirmation for your records.

7

Submit and Save

Review the filled form carefully, click Submit, and print or save a copy of your application form and payment confirmation. You will receive a confirmation email with your registration ID.

⚠️ Registration Errors to Avoid
Use a valid, active email ID and mobile number — keep them active till the final result date for all communications from IIM.
Choose your test city carefully — once the exam centre is assigned, you cannot change it.
Select IIMs wisely in the form. You will only be eligible for the WAT-PI process of the IIMs you select.
Enter your academic scores for 10th, 12th, graduation accurately. Incorrect data can lead to disqualification at the interview stage.
Upload valid caste / PwD / Scribe certificates — failure to upload them results in rejection of your reserved category claim.
Ensure your fee payment is confirmed and check for an email acknowledgment — without this, your admit card will not be issued.
❓ Frequently Asked Questions — CAT 2026
When will CAT 2026 notification be released?
CAT 2026 notification is typically released in July or August. The official announcement is made on iimcat.ac.in. Bookmark this page — we will update it as soon as the notification is out.
Which IIM will conduct CAT 2026?
CAT 2026 is expected to be conducted by IIM Kozhikode. Each year, a different IIM acts as the conducting body on a rotational basis.
Is there any age restriction for CAT 2026?
No. There is no upper age limit for the CAT exam. Candidates of any age can appear as long as they meet the academic eligibility criteria.
Can final year students apply for CAT 2026?
Yes. Final year undergraduate students can apply for CAT 2026, subject to approval from their institution's Principal or Registrar. They must complete their degree before joining any IIM programme.
How many times is CAT conducted in a year?
CAT is conducted once a year, typically in November. It is held in 3 slots on a single day across multiple cities in India.
In how many slots is CAT conducted and which slot is most difficult?
CAT is conducted in 3 slots — morning, afternoon, and evening. The difficulty level may vary slightly across slots, but all percentile scores are normalised to ensure fairness.
