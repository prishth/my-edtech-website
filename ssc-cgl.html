🎯 SSC CGL 2026 Preparation Strategy
1
Prioritise Tier 1 Accuracy: With 15-minute sectional timing introduced in 2026, you cannot compensate weak sections with strong ones. Practice all 4 sections equally.
2
General Awareness is a Score Booster: GA requires the least time per question but has high scoring potential. Read a standard GK book and follow daily current affairs for 3 months.
3
Build QA Shortcuts: SSC CGL Quant questions follow predictable patterns. Learn Vedic math tricks and short-cut formulas — they can save 30+ seconds per question.
4
English RC Strategy: Read one RC passage daily from quality newspapers. SSC English passages are easier than CAT but require speed and accuracy.
5
Attempt Mock Tests in Sectional Mode: Since Tier 1 has sectional time limits, always practice mocks with individual section timers — never attempt in free mode.
❓ Frequently Asked Questions — SSC CGL 2026
What is the SSC CGL 2026 vacancy count?
The SSC CGL 2026 notification announced 12,256 (tentative) vacancies across various Group B and Group C posts in central government departments.
Is there negative marking in SSC CGL?
Yes. Tier 1 has a negative marking of 0.50 marks for each wrong answer. Sectional timing of 15 minutes per section is now implemented.
What posts can I get through SSC CGL?
SSC CGL recruits for posts such as Assistant Audit Officer, Assistant Accounts Officer, Inspector of Income Tax, Sub-Inspector (CBI), Statistical Investigator, Assistant in CSS, and many others.
What is the SSC CGL selection process?
SSC CGL 2026 has two tiers: Tier 1 (shortlisting) and Tier 2 (final merit ranking). There is no interview. Final selection is based on Tier 2 marks and post preferences.
What is the salary for SSC CGL posts?
Salary ranges from ₹25,500 to ₹1,51,100 per month depending on the post, with additional benefits like HRA, TA, and medical facilities.
