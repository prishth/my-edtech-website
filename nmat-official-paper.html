📋What Is the NMAT Exam?

NMAT by GMAC is a computerised adaptive test that measures competency in three core areas:

Language SkillsQuantitative SkillsLogical Reasoning

Each section is timed individually, and the test adapts to your performance, serving questions based on the accuracy of your previous answers. That adaptive behaviour is what makes NMAT challenging — the difficulty level is customised to you, so two candidates never sit the same paper.

🎯Why the Official Papers Are Worth Your Time

  • Real exam formatThe practice papers follow the exact NMAT structure, including section-wise time limits and the adaptive character of the test.
  • Full range of question typesFrom multiple-choice questions in Language Skills to multi-step problem-solving in Quantitative Skills.

✅How to Use These Papers

Attempt each paper in one sitting with a hard timer on every section, exactly as the real exam runs. Do not pause between sections and do not go back. The point is to rehearse the constraint, not just the content.

Afterwards, review every question you guessed as well as every question you got wrong. On an exam with no negative marking, the guesses tell you as much about your preparation as the errors do.

❓Frequently Asked Questions — NMAT Official Papers

Are these NMAT practice papers free?

Yes. You can download every NMAT practice paper on this page in PDF form for free, with no signup.

Do the NMAT practice papers simulate the actual exam?

Yes. These papers are designed to closely mirror the actual NMAT, including question types and the adaptive format of the test.

Can I download practice papers for previous years?

Yes. Papers are listed year wise and can be downloaded from the table above.

How do these papers help improve my score?

Practising with official papers builds familiarity with the exam format, improves your speed and sharpens accuracy — all of which feed directly into a better score.

Is there a limit to how many times I can take NMAT?

You can take NMAT up to three times within a testing cycle. Participating schools consider your best score.

When is the NMAT 2026 exam?

The NMAT 2026 exam window runs from November to December 2026. Registration opens on 1 August 2026.

Are calculators allowed during the NMAT exam?

No. Calculators are not permitted. All calculations must be done manually, so practise without one.
